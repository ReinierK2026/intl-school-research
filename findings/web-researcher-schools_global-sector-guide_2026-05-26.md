## Executive Summary

As of May 2026 the global K-12 international school sector numbers **15,075 schools, 7.7 million students and US$69.3 billion in annual fee income** — sustaining a six-year expansion trajectory (8% school growth, 13% enrolment growth) even as its risk profile sharpens materially. The dominant external shock is geopolitical: USAID's dissolution in early 2025 and US immigration restrictions (travel ban expanded Jan 2026, 20% drop in new international students at US institutions by Spring 2026) are producing enrollment and staff-pipeline disruptions that ripple from higher education into K-12 international schools serving expat communities worldwide. Simultaneously, **60% of international teachers have considered leaving the profession due to burnout**, private equity now controls ~40% of all schools, and ESG compliance is transitioning from competitive differentiator to investibility baseline.
