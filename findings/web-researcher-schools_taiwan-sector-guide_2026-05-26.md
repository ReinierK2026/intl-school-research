## Executive Summary

Taiwan's international school market is small, supply-constrained, and dominated by a handful of well-established institutions serving a Western expat community of approximately **21,000 people** — a uniquely concentrated risk profile. Taipei American School (TAS), the sector's flagship, enrols ~2,400 students and commands one of Asia's highest first-year fee packages (~US$41,000). The overriding risk as of May 2026 is **geopolitical**: cross-strait tensions have reached their most fraught level since 2022, with 202 Chinese military aircraft detected around Taiwan in April 2026, the largest PLA war game in three years conducted in December 2025, and US support increasingly uncertain under the Trump II administration. A rapid, unplanned expat departure event — triggered by military escalation or a major multinational evacuation order — would be the sector's existential scenario. Outside this tail risk, Taiwan's international schools are operationally stable, lightly regulated (relative to China), and benefit from competitive fee positioning compared to Singapore, Hong Kong and Tokyo.
