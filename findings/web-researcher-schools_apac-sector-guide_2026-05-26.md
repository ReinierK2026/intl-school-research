## Executive Summary

Asia-Pacific hosts **58% of global international schools** and remains the dominant growth region, but the market is bifurcating sharply. Southeast Asia and Western Asia (Gulf) are growing strongly while East Asia — led by a stalling China — is flatlining. China's regulatory tightening (no new international schools for compulsory education years; overseas textbook bans; 972 accredited schools with restricted enrolment rules) contrasts with Southeast Asia's booming demand and the Gulf's aggressive new-build pipeline. The US immigration shock and USAID closure are creating both risk (reduced American expat/teacher inflow) and opportunity (APAC institutions absorbing students diverted from US institutions, with 82% of APAC higher-education institutions recording international enrollment growth). Teacher shortage in Southeast Asia alone stands at **4.5 million**, making the regional talent pipeline the most acute near-term operational constraint.
